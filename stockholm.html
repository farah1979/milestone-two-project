Visit Stockholm

The capital of Sweden, Stockholm looks prettyunusual. Multi-colored houses, colored like a child, tightly pressed against each other. And if you want you can take a walk on the roof, like the world famous kid from tales of Astrid Lindgren. Stockholm is especially beautiful in the spring. It was at this time, daffodils bloom, and at home in the spring sunshine look particularly beautiful.

Why Stockholm is the best Scandinavian capital?

Sweden is a country that I’ve always held in such high regard. It could be because of the overall friendliness of the citizens, their excellent ability to speak perfect english, the chic style they have grown accustomed to wearing, their love of nature and abundance of green spaces, etc. but the draw seems to be something more than the sum of all of these parts. When compared to other cities in the world, its size by population is humbling (around 2.5 million people in the metropolitan area), and yet each island that make up this 14 island archipelago of a capital, has something unique to offer. You can find in the Google map some important places Which I was able to show them with some coordinates at the end of this post.The easiest way to get around Stockholm, is by using public transport. Stockholm boasts an excellent metro system that is extremely easy to navigate, called the Tunnelbana. Virtually every station has a different artistic element to it, usually in the form of murals and exhibitions. This makes the Tunnelbana the longest art exhibit in the world. Luckily, instead of having to stop at every single station in the Tunnelbana, Storstockholms Lokaltrafik, or SL for short, gives free guided tours of the art in a select few stations throughout the year. All you need is a metro ticket to access the Tunnelbana. The stations at Rådhuset and T-Centralen were my favorites to photograph.

Sunny walks with beautiful views

Mariaberget – Skinnarviksberget

A walk along this "mountain" on northern Södermalm gives you a spectacular view of almost the entire inner city. It is a short but beautiful stretch that impresses most visitors thanks to its postcard-beautiful views and picturesque architecture.

Riddarfjärden around

This round is best suited during the winter because it is completely without shade. A smart choice when half the year is spent chasing the sun's rays, that is. The trick to getting the most out of a walk around Riddarholmen is to be up early and choose a clear day.I usually start in Hornstull because I lived there before, but you can actually start anywhere along Riddarfjärden. If you start from Hornstull, there are plenty of cafés to buy a coffee from. Aim for the Västerbron and be prepared for a view (and occasionally even wind) that will take your breath away.

Skogskyrkogården

Take the green line to the metro station Skogskyrkogården. I know you are eager to walk, but the road there is long and sometimes heavily trafficked. On site you will experience a cemetery with incredible architecture and a peaceful feeling of rest and inner peace is promised. One person who got his last rest here is a real local, Greta Garbo. Her grave is not at all as secretive as she was in life, so it is quite possible to find the place if you want.When you feel finished with Skogskyrkogården, you can key in the GPS at Matateljén or Gamla Enskede Matbod, which gives you a 25-minute walk through the picturesque surroundings of Gamla Enskede before you finish with a local piece of food.

Skinnarviksberget

Central Stockholm's highest natural point with magnificent views of, among other places, Kungsholmen, the Old Town and Stockholm City Hall. The park below is a gem for a nice picnic. The small café is open in good weather and is always well visited when the sun is up. Good sandwiches, coffee and ice cream are of course served here.

A place of strange beauty and poetry. Laid over a period of a quarter of a century (1915–1940) on an area of 100 hectares on a pine-covered pebble ridge, under the direction of the famous modernist architects Gunnar Asplund and Sigurd Lewerentz.
