Gallary

Nobel Prize Museum

The Nobel Museum or Nobel Prize Museum is a museum about the Nobel Prize and the Nobel Laureates, located in Börshuset on Stortorget 2 in the Old Town, Stockholm, inaugurated in 2001 in connection with the 100th anniversary of the Nobel Prize. The boss is Erika Lanner. The museum changed its name to the Nobel Prize Museum from the Nobel Museum on January 21, 2019.

Drottningholms Slott

Drottningholm Castle is a royal castle located on Lovön in Lake Mälaren, in Ekerö municipality west of Stockholm. King Carl XVI Gustaf and Queen Silvia have had their official residence in the southern part of the castle since 1981. The castle got its name as early as 1579, during the time of Johan III. He had a stone house built for his queen Katarina.

Djurgarden

Djurgården or Södra Djurgården is an island and a district in Stockholm, and is part of the Royal National City Park. During his time as an island, Djurgården has also been called Valdemarsön. Djurgården is known for its many sights and for its nature with many old oaks. Urban buildings are located in the southwestern part.

ABBA The Museum

The Abba Museum is a privately owned permanent exhibition about the Swedish music group Abba located in the Konsthallen district at Djurgårdsvägen 68 on Djurgården in Stockholm. The Abba Museum is located within the Royal National City Park. Until 2017, it also shared the building and entrance with the museum for the music initiative Swedish Music Hall of Fame.

Gröna Lund

Gröna Lund, also known as Grönan, is an amusement park located on Djurgården, Stockholm, with the main entrance from Allmänna gränd. Gröna Lund is located within the Royal National City Park. In the park there are about thirty rides, including 7 roller coasters, haunted houses, most games and wheel of fortune, snacks and fast food kiosks as well as restaurants and bars. There are also two stages and a dance floor and annually over 60 concerts and also many dance evenings are held.

Kungliga slottet

Stockholm Castle, officially the Royal Palace, has been located on the same site by Norrström on northeastern Stadsholmen in Stockholm since the latter half of the 13th century. Its first-century history belongs to the medieval castle complex Tre Kronor. In modern times, the term refers to the building called the Royal Palace, one of the royal castles in the country located in the Old Town district of Stockholm.

Skansen

Skansen is a foundation-owned Swedish open-air museum with a zoo on Djurgården in Stockholm, founded by Artur Hazelius, and is located within the Royal National City Park. The fort was inaugurated on October 11, 1891. The name comes from the fort that Karl XIV Johan had built for his son Oscar I to be used as a playground, this fort was next to the current Håsjö pillar.

Stockholms stadshus

Stockholm City Hall is located on the old fire station site at Hantverkargatan 1 on Kungsholmen in central Stockholm. Stockholm City Council decided in 1908 to have a municipal building with a party floor built on this plot towards Lake Mälaren, after an earlier proposal for a new town hall with town hall rights did not materialize. Architect Ragnar Östberg was commissioned to design the lavish building.

Waipo Stockholmd

Genuine food from China's dominant food provinces and modern service in the form of iPads built into the tables. Ardbeg Embassy

Ardbeg Embassy is located in the Old Town and has a unique restaurant concept rooted in Scotch malt whiskey and Swedish microbreweries on tap. In 2015, Ardbeg Embassy was named Sweden's best whiskey restaurant according to the Swedish Whiskey Association, and is also the only restaurant in the world that serves Scotch whiskey directly on tap.
